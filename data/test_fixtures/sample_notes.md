# Pharmacology Study Notes

This document covers essential pharmacology concepts for medical education.

## Cardiovascular Pharmacology

### ACE Inhibitors

ACE inhibitors (Angiotensin-Converting Enzyme inhibitors) work by blocking the conversion of angiotensin I to angiotensin II. This leads to vasodilation and reduced blood pressure.

Common ACE inhibitors include:
- Lisinopril 5-40mg daily
- Enalapril 5-20mg twice daily
- Ramipril 2.5-10mg daily

**Side Effects:**
- Dry cough (due to bradykinin accumulation)
- Hyperkalemia (K+ > 5.5 mEq/L)
- Angioedema (rare but serious)
- First-dose hypotension

### Beta-Blockers

Beta-blockers work by blocking beta-adrenergic receptors. They reduce heart rate and blood pressure.

**Beta-1 Selective:**
- Metoprolol 25-100mg twice daily
- Atenolol 50-100mg daily

**Non-Selective:**
- Propranolol 40-160mg twice daily
- Carvedilol 6.25-25mg twice daily

### Mechanism of Action

Beta-1 receptor blockade reduces:
- Heart rate (negative chronotropy)
- Contractility (negative inotropy)
- Blood pressure

Beta-2 receptor blockade can cause:
- Bronchoconstriction (avoid in asthma)
- Peripheral vasoconstriction

## Diuretics

### Loop Diuretics

Furosemide (Lasix) 20-80mg inhibits the Na-K-2Cl cotransporter in the thick ascending loop of Henle. This produces potent diuresis.

**Clinical Uses:**
- Acute pulmonary edema
- Congestive heart failure
- Hypertension (resistant)

### Thiazide Diuretics

Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) 12.5-50mg inhibits the Na-Cl cotransporter in the distal convoluted tubule.

**Side Effects:**
- Hypokalemia (K+ < 3.5 mEq/L)
- Hyperuricemia
- Hypercalcemia
- Hyperglycemia

## Drug Interactions

### Important Drug Interactions to Remember

1. ACE inhibitors + K+-sparing diuretics = Hyperkalemia risk
2. Beta-blockers + Non-DHP calcium channel blockers = Bradycardia risk
3. Loop diuretics + Aminoglycosides = Ototoxicity risk

## Summary

Understanding cardiovascular pharmacology is essential for treating:
- Hypertension (HTN)
- Congestive heart failure (CHF)
- Coronary artery disease (CAD)
- Arrhythmias
